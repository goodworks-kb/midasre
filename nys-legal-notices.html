New York State Legal Notices

Last Updated: January 2024

IMPORTANT: The following notices are required by New York State law for all real estate brokers and agents operating within the state.

Fair Housing Notice - New York State Human Rights Law

Midas Realty is committed to compliance with the New York State Human Rights Law and the Federal Fair Housing Act. We provide equal housing opportunities to all persons regardless of:

  • Race or Color: We do not discriminate based on race, color, or ethnic background.
  • Religion: We serve clients of all religious beliefs and backgrounds.
  • Sex or Gender: We provide equal service regardless of sex, gender identity, or gender expression.
  • Disability: We make reasonable accommodations for persons with disabilities and do not discriminate based on disability status.
  • Familial Status: We serve families with children, including families with children under 18, pregnant women, and persons in the process of securing custody of children.
  • National Origin: We serve clients regardless of their country of origin, ancestry, or citizenship status.
  • Age: We do not discriminate based on age (except as permitted by law for senior housing).
  • Marital Status: We serve single, married, divorced, widowed, and domestic partnership clients equally.
  • Sexual Orientation: We provide equal service to all clients regardless of sexual orientation.
  • Military Status: We honor and serve active military personnel and veterans.

It is illegal to discriminate in the sale, rental, or financing of housing based on any of these protected characteristics.

Reasonable Accommodation Notice

Midas Realty is committed to providing reasonable accommodations to persons with disabilities to ensure equal access to our services and properties.

What is a Reasonable Accommodation?

A reasonable accommodation is a change, exception, or adjustment to a rule, policy, practice, or service that may be necessary for a person with a disability to have an equal opportunity to use and enjoy a dwelling, including public and common use spaces.

Examples of Reasonable Accommodations:

  • Allowing a service animal or emotional support animal in a property with a "no pets" policy
  • Reserving a parking space close to the entrance for a person with mobility impairments
  • Providing documents in alternative formats (large print, Braille, audio) for persons with visual impairments
  • Allowing modifications to a rental unit to accommodate a disability (with appropriate agreements for restoration)
  • Providing sign language interpreters or other communication assistance for persons with hearing impairments
  • Adjusting application procedures or deadlines for persons with disabilities

Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

Midas Realty maintains Standard Operating Procedures to ensure consistent, fair, and lawful practices in all real estate transactions. Our SOPs include:

1. Client Representation and Disclosure

We clearly disclose our agency relationships (buyer's agent, seller's agent, dual agent, or transaction broker) in writing before entering into any representation agreement. All clients receive a copy of our agency disclosure form and have the opportunity to ask questions.

2. Fair Housing Compliance

All agents and staff are trained on fair housing laws and are required to:

  • Treat all clients and potential clients equally regardless of protected characteristics
  • Use inclusive language in all marketing materials and communications
  • Show all available properties that meet a client's stated criteria
  • Document all property showings and client interactions
  • Report any suspected fair housing violations immediately

3. Property Information and Disclosures

We provide accurate property information to the best of our knowledge and:

  • Disclose all known material defects and issues
  • Provide required property disclosure forms
  • Advise clients to conduct independent inspections
  • Maintain records of all disclosures and communications

4. Reasonable Accommodation Procedures

When a reasonable accommodation is requested:

  1. We acknowledge the request within 48 hours
  2. We review the request and determine if it is reasonable and necessary
  3. We may request documentation if needed to verify the need
  4. We provide a written response within 10 business days
  5. We implement approved accommodations promptly
  6. We maintain records of all accommodation requests and responses

5. Complaint Handling

We maintain a complaint resolution process:

  • All complaints are documented and investigated promptly
  • Clients receive written acknowledgment of complaints within 5 business days
  • We work to resolve complaints fairly and expeditiously
  • Clients are informed of their right to file complaints with regulatory agencies

Filing a Complaint

If you believe you have been discriminated against or that your rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with:

  • New York State Division of Human Rights: 1-888-392-3644 or www.dhr.ny.gov
  • U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD): 1-800-669-9777 or www.hud.gov/fairhousing
  • New York State Department of State, Division of Licensing Services: (518) 474-4429 or www.dos.ny.gov

Complaints must be filed within one year of the alleged discrimination or violation.
